Pascal: The Helicopter Parent of Programming

The Great Code Cleanup of 1970

The situation: 1960s programming was like texting without autocorrect - chaotic and full of mistakes


Meet Niklaus Wirth: Swiss perfectionist who looked at messy code and said "Nein! This will not do!"


The vibe: Pascal was like that friend who color-codes their closet and actually follows through

Pascal in Action

program MillennialLife; var coffeeCount: integer; rentMoney: real; willSurvive: boolean; begin coffeeCount := 5; rentMoney := 2000.00; if (coffeeCount > 3) and (rentMoney > 1500) then willSurvive := false else willSurvive := true; writeln('Millennial survival status: ', willSurvive); end.

Clean, organized, and it actually tells you what it's doing!

The Time Loop: Why Everything Old is New Again

The Great Circle of Tech Life

  • 1960s: "Make it readable!" (COBOL)
  • 1970s: "Make it organized!" (Pascal)
  • 1980s-90s: "Make it flexible!" (Chaos ensues)
  • 2000s: "Wait, why does everything break?" (Add types back)
  • 2010s: "Let's make simple, reliable languages!" (Rediscover Pascal's ideas)
  • 2020s: "Why is our infrastructure so complex?" (COBOL looking smug)
